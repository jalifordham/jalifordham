John Berger Presents:

"A New Way of Seeing"

The way we perceive the things we see such as art, in fact, especially art depends heavily on our backgrounds and the norms of our cultures and societies. We process what we see based on our own experiences, in some ways, this can make our ideas about what certain art and paintings are about very arbitrary.
Berger suggests that viewing art and learning art history has been overtly mystified in order to compensate for the loss of sacredness that inevitably happened with the invention of the camera and new abilities to replicate and view art from anywhere.
