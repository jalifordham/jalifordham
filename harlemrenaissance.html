Harlem Renaissance

Background

Low wages, lawfully accepted segregation, lack of available schooling, devastating floods, and blatant racism drove Black people to flee the southern states up to the north in America. Many from the Caribbean Islands followed, seeking social and economic prosperity that was promised by the industrial cities in the north unable to find workers due to the ban on travel to the states from Europe. The movement was dubbed the Great Negro Migration and later rebranded the Great Migration, beginning during the First World War, surging during 1910-1916 persisting through to the 1940s. The movement was dubbed the Great Negro Migration and later rebranded the Great Migration became what we know as the Harlem Renaissance. beginning during the First World War, surging during 1910-1916 persisting through to the 1940s. Aside from being categorized as the time that the “negros made it into vogue” (3, The Harlem Renaissance), The Harlem Renaissance was the first movement in western art that engaged Black intellectuals, poets, novelists, musicians, painters and sculptors, and performers. It was the emergence of blues and jazz in the north, innovative art, and urban culture and paved the way for Black modernism.
